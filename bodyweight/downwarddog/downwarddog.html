Downward Dog Pose (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

How to Perform the Downward Dog Pose

The Downward Dog Pose, or Adho Mukha Svanasana, is a foundational yoga pose that stretches the entire body, strengthens the arms and legs, and relieves stress. It is commonly used as a transition pose or as part of a warm-up sequence.

Step 1: Begin on All Fours

Start on your hands and knees, with your wrists aligned under your shoulders and your knees under your hips. Spread your fingers wide and press firmly into the ground.

Step 2: Lift Your Hips Up

As you exhale, lift your knees off the floor and raise your hips towards the ceiling. Straighten your legs as much as possible without locking your knees. Your body should form an inverted V shape.

Step 3: Stretch and Hold

Press your heels down towards the mat (it's okay if they don't touch the floor). Lengthen your spine, relax your head between your arms, and gaze towards your navel or thighs. Hold the pose for 5-10 breaths.

Step 4: Return to All Fours

Inhale as you gently lower your knees back to the floor, returning to the starting position on all fours. Take a moment to rest before moving into your next pose.
